Mimicorp

A field lab for research, systems work, and public-facing projects.

Mimicorp functions as a lab where research, digital systems, media, and narrative experimentation can be developed in relation rather than in isolation. It is both a working studio and an organizing structure for projects that move between inquiry, interpretation, and public form.

The lab holds work across research writing, computational interpretation, audio, documentary form, and site-based publication. Its purpose is to stage systems clearly, track evolving lines of thought, and make room for both rigorous and experimental outputs.

Lab Description

Research, narrative, and systems in one frame.

Mimicorp is structured to hold work that crosses disciplinary boundaries without flattening them into a single format or audience.

Amelia Cronan

Operator, researcher, and builder across the Mimicorp system.

Amelia Cronan works across communication, digital systems, research design, and creative production. Her background spans e-commerce, nonprofit growth, campaigns, public art, agriculture, analytics, and media, allowing her to move between practical execution and conceptual framing with continuity.

Her formal background includes an M.A. in Political Science from the University of Washington, a B.A. in Political Science and German from Loyola University New Orleans, additional technical coursework, and long experience building projects that require both organization and interpretive judgment.
