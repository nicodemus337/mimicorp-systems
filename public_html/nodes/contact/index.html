About Amelia

Strategy, storytelling, and systems design built through Mimicorp.

Amelia Cronan is a marketing director, campaign strategist, and creative operator with more than a decade of experience building initiatives that turn attention into measurable outcomes. Through Mimicorp, she brings together digital systems, documentary thinking, behavioral insight, and place-based observation to help complex projects become more legible, more human, and easier to support.

Biography

Amelia works across marketing, media, advocacy, and land-based storytelling.

Her background spans e-commerce, nonprofit growth, political campaigns, public art, regenerative agriculture, and creative direction. Trained in political science and behavioral science methodologies, she builds campaigns and communication systems that connect story, audience behavior, and operational outcomes.

At Gonsoulin Land and Cattle, Amelia designed and launched a digital marketing system that grew online sales from roughly $1,000 to more than $180,000 annually, turning e-commerce into the ranch's leading revenue stream. That work combines brand storytelling, funnel design, grant writing, AI-assisted analytics, and a deep commitment to regenerative agriculture and community food security.

Through Mimicorp, she also leads creative campaigns, documentary media, and strategy work rooted in South Louisiana. That includes public art, nonprofit consulting, local political campaigns, and Second Cutting, a documentary series focused on land, people, and regenerative agricultural life.

Amelia's current work also includes The Ecological Archive, which she co-founded and where she serves on the Board of Directors as Secretary, helping shape governance, organizational growth, and long-term mission alignment.

Credentials

Grounded in measurable growth, multilingual communication, and long-horizon cultural work.

Amelia brings 10+ years of experience in strategic marketing, campaign architecture, funnel design, grant development, and stakeholder engagement across commercial, nonprofit, political, and creative sectors.

Her formal background includes an M.A. in Political Science from the University of Washington, a B.A. in Political Science and German from Loyola University New Orleans, computer science and electrical engineering coursework at the University of Louisiana at Lafayette, and CompTIA A+ certification.

She works fluently across English and German, with strong Cajun French, French, and Russian language abilities, and brings formal music training in piano and guitar into media, educational, and narrative work when it strengthens the message.
