Method

Field, Media, and Systems Method

The research at Mimicorp Systems is built through contact rather than distance. It moves between political theory, field observation, seasonal attention, media production, and infrastructural analysis in order to keep concepts answerable to the world they claim to describe.

Field Observation

Repeated attention to land, weather, animals, maintenance, and working routines provides more than local color. It shows how systems actually behave under pressure and what forms of intelligence emerge when people have to keep them going.

Comparative Description

Institutional language is read against what can be observed, heard, and tracked over time. The method relies on comparison: policy against practice, record against landscape, schedule against season, category against consequence.

Media Production

Audio, notes, and visual documentation are treated as part of the inquiry itself. They preserve rhythm, speech, atmosphere, and contradiction in ways that help resist the flattening tendencies of official or academic prose.

Situated Judgment

The project does not assume that detachment produces the best account. Instead, it asks how judgment becomes more precise when it remains exposed to consequence, revision, and the need to describe things clearly enough for someone else to act.

Working Commitments

  • Stay close to material conditions before converting them into theory.
  • Treat maintenance and ordinary labor as analytically significant.
  • Use media to preserve what abstract description leaves out.
  • Return to the same sites often enough for pattern and drift to appear.
  • Let field contact revise the conceptual frame rather than merely illustrate it.
