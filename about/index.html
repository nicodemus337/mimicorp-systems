About mimicorp labs

mimicorp labs is the working platform of a social scientist operating across research, media, and production. The site is organized around the conviction that governance, ecology, and lived experience are inseparable in practice, and that serious public work should be able to move between concept, field observation, and production without losing rigor.

The work is grounded in land, governance, and lived systems, with an emphasis on how authority is staged through time, infrastructure, language, and ordinary life. Rather than separating analysis from encounter, mimicorp labs treats writing, fieldwork, and media as mutually informing forms of inquiry.

Research

Dissertation development and long-form writing on governance, time, ecological systems, performance, and judgment.

Production

Media work that documents systems from the inside through field notes, audio, and observational formats grounded in place.

Biographical Notes

The research centers on governance as a lived and staged reality: how systems become believable, how institutional language drifts from material conditions, and how judgment survives when inherited procedures no longer fully describe the world.

The method is intentionally cross-disciplinary. It draws together political theory, field observation, agricultural attention, media production, and systems analysis, allowing the field to revise the conceptual frame rather than merely illustrate it.

Across dissertation work, field notes, and audio projects like Second Cutting, the throughline is the same: to build forms of public language that remain answerable to land, consequence, and lived experience.

Orientation

The method behind the work is cross-disciplinary but not diffuse. It treats institutions, land, and infrastructure as overlapping realities and asks how authority is made to appear credible in ordinary life.

Rather than separating analysis from experience, mimicorp labs uses the friction between them as a source of knowledge. The result is work that is both conceptual and field-attentive.
