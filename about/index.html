Biographical Notes

The research centers on governance as a lived and staged reality: how systems become believable, how institutional language drifts from material conditions, and how judgment survives when inherited procedures no longer fully describe the world.

The method is intentionally cross-disciplinary. It draws together political theory, field observation, agricultural attention, media production, and systems analysis, allowing the field to revise the conceptual frame rather than merely illustrate it.

Across dissertation work, field notes, websites, project systems, and audio projects like Second Cutting, the throughline is the same: to build forms of public language and practical infrastructure that remain answerable to land, consequence, and lived experience.

Orientation

The method behind the work is cross-disciplinary but not diffuse. It treats institutions, land, websites, workflows, and infrastructure as overlapping realities and asks how credibility is made, lost, repaired, and maintained in ordinary life.

Rather than separating analysis from execution, mimicorp labs uses the friction between them as a source of knowledge. The result is work that is conceptual, field-attentive, and useful when something has to be fixed or delivered.
